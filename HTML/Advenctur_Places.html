Mount Abu

Mount Abu is a hill station in the Aravalli Range in the Sirohi district of the state of Rajasthan in western India. The mountain forms a rocky plateau 22 km long by 9 km wide. The highest peak on the mountain is Guru Shikhar at 1,722 m (5,650 ft) above sea level. It is referred to as 'an oasis in the desert' as its heights are home to rivers, lakes, waterfalls and evergreen forests. It is also home to numerous Hindu and Jain temples. The nearest train station is Abu Road railway station 28 km away.

Bundi, a hidden gem tucked away in the heart of Rajasthan, offers a unique blend of history and adventure. Nestled amidst the rugged terrain of the Aravalli Range, Bundi presents an array of thrilling experiences for the adventurous soul.
Adventurers in Bundi can embark on exhilarating rock climbing expeditions, scaling the ancient fort walls that overlook the town and surrounding landscape. Rappelling down the cliffs offers a heart-pounding descent amidst stunning vistas of the picturesque Bundi countryside.

In Bundi, adventure awaits around every corner, promising an unforgettable experience for those who dare to explore its hidden treasures.
