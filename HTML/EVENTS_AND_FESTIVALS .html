DESERT FESTIVALS

Desert Festival in Jaisalmer is an annual event of three days held in the month of Magh (February), before the full moon with pomp and pride. The last day falls on the full moon day. This year the Desert Festival will be held on 22- 24 February. The festival has various programmes placed at different venues in and around Jaisalmer concluding at Sam Dunes which is 42 km from the city of Jaisalmer.

Jaisalmer Desert Festival is one of its kind and takes you on a mesmerizing journey of experiencing the city‚Äôs rich cultural heritage of performing arts, local artistry, beautiful wood carvings, and lip-smacking regional delicacies. MEWAR FESTIVALS

The Marwar Festival, celebrated in the Marwar region of Rajasthan, India, is a vibrant showcase of the rich cultural heritage of the area. It usually takes place in the city of Jodhpur, the cultural capital of Marwar. The festival is a two-day event held annually in the month of Ashwin (September-October), during the full moon.

Marwar Festival is primarily dedicated to showcasing the art, music, dance, and traditions of Rajasthan, particularly those of the Marwar region. It offers visitors a glimpse into the royal past of Rajasthan, with various cultural events and performances held against the backdrop of historical monuments like Mehrangarh Fort and Umaid Bhawan Palace.

KUMBHALGARH FESTIVALS

Rajasthan is well known among the global travelers for its rich culture and tradition, a truly blissful one to enjoy and explore. Kumbhalgarh is one such festival commemorating the patronage of Maharana Kumbh to arts and culture during his reign.

The three days festival includes lot of fun filled activities, trade shows and performances by artistes. It is so appealing to have all categories of shows under one roof that too with the elegant backdrop of Kumbhalagrh fort, which is acclaimed to be the second largest next to the great wall of China.

The day‚Äôs schedule is in two sections. One is for encouraging the native artisans to showcase their talents in the form of jewelry, ethnic wear, handicrafts and other things. The second section is usually during the evenings when the audience are entertained with various cultural shows. The events are so engaging and vibrant that many times the onlookers join the club to stage their talents.

RANAKPUR FESTIVALS

Ranakpur, located in the Pali district of Rajasthan, is famous for its intricate Jain temples, particularly the renowned Ranakpur Jain Temple. This architectural marvel, built in the 15th century, serves as the backdrop for the festival.

The Ranakpur Festival is usually held in December or January, coinciding with the winter season when the weather is pleasant in Rajasthan.

The festival is a vibrant celebration of Jain culture, music, dance, and traditions. It attracts both locals and tourists who come to witness the grandeur of the temple and partake in the festivities.

During the festival, the temple premises come alive with various cultural performances, including classical music concerts, folk dances, and recitals. Renowned artists from across India showcase their talent, adding to the festive ambiance.
